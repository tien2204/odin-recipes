Pancetta pasta with cream sauce

Description

Pancetta pasta is an Italian pasta dish flavored with rich, salty pancetta. Pancetta is seasoned, salt-cured meat cut from the pork belly (the pig’s underside). Pancetta is light pink with a dense, silky texture and nutty flavor. You’ll find it sold in thin slices in Italy and most oftenly cubed in the United States.

Ingredients

  • 8 ounces dried pasta (such as fettucini, penne, linguine, or rigatoni)
  • 4 ounces pancetta, cubed
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 shallot, minced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • ¼ cup Parmesan or pecorino cheese, grated
  • ½ cup heavy cream
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • ¼ teaspoon red pepper flakes, optional

Steps

  1. Bring a large pot of water to a boil over high heat and salt the water.
  2. While the water is coming to a boil, heat a large skillet on the stove over medium heat.
  3. Add the cubed pancetta to the skillet and sauté until crispy, about 10 minutes.
  4. Add the pasta to the boiling, salted water and cook for 2 minutes less than the package directions
  5. While the pasta cooks, remove the crispy pancetta from the pan.
  6. Add the olive oil to the rendered pork fat.
  7. Add the minced shallot and minced garlic to the pan and sauté for 5 minutes.
  8. Lower the heat under the skillet to low heat and add the crispy pancetta, cheese, and heavy cream.
  9. Using tongs, transfer the al dente pasta from the large pot to the skillet with the sauce. Some pasta water will get into the sauce.
  10. Increase the heat the medium heat and bring the sauce to a simmer while tossing the pasta.
  11. Season the pasta with salt, pepper, and optional red pepper flakes.
  12. Once the sauce is thick, and the pasta fully cooked, serve the pasta hot and garnish with extra grated cheese.
